# Narrative Architect Skill

## Purpose
Transform a structured story intent into a compelling 5-scene narrative optimized for
multimodal presentation (text + images + audio).

## Scene Structure Patterns

### Chronological
1. **Opening**: Set the time/place. Establish the world before the event.
2. **Rising Action**: Introduce the central conflict or turning point.
3. **Crisis**: The peak of tension. Everything is at stake.
4. **Turning Point**: The pivotal moment. Something changes forever.
5. **Resolution**: Show the aftermath. What did we learn? What changed?

### Hero's Journey
1. **Ordinary World**: Establish the hero in their normal life.
2. **Call to Adventure**: The inciting event disrupts everything.
3. **Trials**: Challenges the hero must overcome.
4. **Transformation**: The hero changes through the ordeal.
5. **Return**: The hero returns changed, bringing wisdom.

### Problem → Solution
1. **The Problem**: Paint the painful reality clearly.
2. **Why It Matters**: Show the human cost, the stakes.
3. **The Search**: What has been tried? What failed?
4. **The Breakthrough**: The insight, the solution, the moment of change.
5. **The Future**: The transformed world. Possibility and hope.

### Poetic
1. **Sensation**: Open with a vivid sensory moment.
2. **Question**: The deep question at the heart of the topic.
3. **Exploration**: Wander through ideas, images, contradictions.
4. **Revelation**: A sudden moment of clarity or beauty.
5. **Echo**: Return to the opening image, transformed by understanding.

## TTS Narration Guidelines
- Use **present tense** ("The crowd surges forward" not "The crowd surged")
- **Short sentences** (max 20 words each). Pause naturally.
- **No jargon** — explain technical terms with metaphor
- **Emotional verbs**: surges, whispers, shatters, blooms, trembles
- **Avoid**: "Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion", "It is worth noting"
- **For children**: Simple words, friendly animals/characters, rhyme optional

## Image Prompt Guidelines
- Always specify: **subject + setting + mood + art style + lighting + colors**
- Examples:
  - Cinematic: "A lone soldier standing in a burning Paris street at dawn, cinematic photography, golden smoke, long shadows, desaturated with amber tones, wide shot"
  - Whimsical: "A tiny green frog wearing a lab coat examining a glowing leaf, cute digital illustration, soft pastel colors, warm sunlight, close-up"
  - Dramatic: "Robespierre at the guillotine, face pale in moonlight, oil painting style, deep shadows, red and black palette, dramatic close-up"
- Never include text in image prompts
- Be specific about style (oil painting / watercolor / photorealistic / digital art / illustration)
